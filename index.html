Max Otto Jones

I am a Cognitive Science major with minors in Neuroscience and Mathematics at Carleton College, where I study neural and hormonal control of social behavior in a snappy Central American rodent under the mentorship of Dr. Joel Tripp. I am also a Research Assistant in the lab of Dr. David Herzfeld at UW-Madison School of Medicine, where I build computational tools to address foundational questions in neuroscience. As a 2026 National Science Foundation SURE-REU Fellow at UW-Madison College of Engineering, co-advised by Dr. Aaron Suminski in Neurological Surgery and Dr. Herzfeld, I lead a cross-department collaboration aiming to uncover how the cerebellum handles uncertainty in sensory feedback.

I believe that the most important questions in neuroscience live at the intersection of physiology, computation, and behavior, and that no single level of analysis is sufficient on its own. A behavior without its mechanism is a story without an explanation; a mechanism without its behavior is a solution without a problem. My aim is to work across these levels with equal rigor, building an integrative understanding to move the field forward. My studies at Carleton are laying that foundation.

I plan to pursue a PhD in Neuroscience or Biomedical Engineering. I am curious about many questions in science. Among the most pertinant of my desires is to understand how we learn, generate, and coordinate movements. I am also generally interested in studying anything that is highly quantifiable and contains important problems to solve. My broder goal is to translate insights from science into solutions to real-world issues.
